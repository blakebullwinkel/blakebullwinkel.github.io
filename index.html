Hi, I'm Blake. I recently completed a master's in Data Science at Harvard's IACS, where I was advised by Pavlos Protopapas. I believe in the magic of machine learning to tackle really hard problems. My thesis focused on using GANs to solve differential equations, but I've also done research in differential privacy and interpretability.

I'm also passionate about applying computational tools to challenges in environmental sustainability and co-founded Marble, an app that brings you carbon footprint estimates for your grocery purchases.

In the spring of 2020, I earned my BA from Williams College, where I double majored in math and Chinese. I spent my junior year studying at the University of Oxford, and before my undergraduate years lived in Hong Kong, Singapore, and Sydney. If you have any questions, feel free to email me.

Projects

Linearized Neural Nets for Transfer Learning with GPs

Implemented the method for transfer learning proposed in Fast Adaptation with Linearized Neural Networks, a 2021 paper by Maddox et al., in TensorFlow and performed experiments to test its practical utility [notebook] [code]

  • Constructed a GP kernel from the Jacobian of a trained neural net for transfer of the network's inductive biases
  • Reproduced results on transfer learning tasks by developing a TensorFlow implementation of the paper's PyTorch model
  • Leveraged toy and real-world data to show that the proposed method works well when the training and adaptation data are similar and the number of adaptation points is small
  • Demonstrated that better predictive performance can often be achieved using GPs with generic RBF kernels

Classifying the Sounds of NYC

Trained and tuned a variety of models to classify audio clips recorded around New York City from the UrbanSound8k dataset into ten different classes [report] [notebook]

  • Performed dimensionality reduction on 1-D amplitude and 2-D mel spectrogram data via PCA
  • Compared vanilla multinomial logistic regression with models that incorporated L2 regularization and class weighting
  • Tuned hyperparameters via grid search to maximize the test accuracies of non-linear models, including k-NN and random forest
  • Trained feed-forward and LSTM networks, experimenting with different model architectures and incorporating regularization using dropout

Modeling ASA Section Membership

Constructed binary response generalized linear models to predict whether or not members of the American Statistical Association belonged to at least one section [report] [code]

  • Built a binary response GLM (logistic regression) model via analysis of deviance and ran model diagnostics, including a Hosmer-Lemeshow test and plots of averaged residuals and Cook's distances
  • Fit a generalized additive model (GAM) with smooths over the continuous predictors to improve model fit
  • Interpreted the effects of the statistically significant GAM predictors and provided recommendations for the ASA that might boost section membership

Forecasting and Classifying Mice Microbiomes

Used deep learning to forecast qPCR time series and classify mouse microbiomes into healthy and infected groups based on data provided by researchers at Brigham and Women's Hospital [notebook]

  • Trained autoregressive and LSTM models to predict the next qPCR reading (total bacteria concentration) based on lagged measurements
  • Used CNNs to predict with 98.05% test accuracy whether mice belonged to the healthy or infected group based on the relative abundances of taxa (ASV) at fixed time steps
  • Bootstrapped the data in order to train LSTMs on the entire history of ASV measurements for the same binary classification task

Woof Woof! Computer Vision & NLP App for Austin Pets Alive

Wildfire Risk Prediction & Response Optimization

I trained tree-based classification models on historical wildfire and weather data to predict the fire risk for a given county and month in California and used mixed-integer programming to determine the optimal assignment of limited firefighters across the state, based on total cost [report] [code]

  • Leveraged minority oversampling (SMOTE) on the training set to address imbalanced classes in the data
  • Tuned random forest hyperparameters (number of estimators and minimum samples per leaf node) using out-of-bag (OOB) error rate
  • Determined the optimal allocation of firefighters in each county and month by using a mixed-integer programming (MIP) solver to encode constraints on the number of available firefighters while minimizing total cost (based on cost of firefighters and fire damages)
  • Evaluated algorithmic bias by comparing the optimized assignment of department-affiliated firefighters to inmate firefighters
